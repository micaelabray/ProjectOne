What is and is not recyclable?

#1 - PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate)
-Recyclable, but should not be reused

#2 - HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene)
-Both reusable and recyclable

#3 – PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride)
-Not recyclable, should not be reused for food/children’s use

#4 – LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene)
-Reusable, but not always recyclable. Please check with your local recycling center to see if they accept LDPE before attempting to recycle.

#5 – PP (Polypropylene)
-Reuseable, but not always recyclable. This plastic is recyclable through some curbside programs, but not all programs accept it.

#6 – PS (Polystyrene)
-Avoid using this plastic. It takes up roughly 35% of the material in US landfills. Recycling for this plastic is not widely available.

#7 – Other (BPA, Polycarbonate and LEXAN)
-Not for reuse unless marked with the PLA compostable coding*.

*PLA compostable coded plastics are not able to be recycled, they need to be composted only.
What are the recyclable plastics? # 1, 2, and 4.
